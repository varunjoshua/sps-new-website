Our Story

Born from a Problem
No One Was Solving

In 1985, S.P. Shenoy — a metallurgical engineer from IIT Bombay — observed a consistent gap in the Indian industrial market: forging shops and steel plants were struggling with scale loss, die wear, and decarburization, but available products were either imported, expensive, or simply not engineered for local conditions.

He founded Steel Plant Specialities in Mumbai to change that. Starting with a focus on anti-scale coatings for hot forging, the company steadily expanded its product range to cover hot rolling, heat treatment, die casting, and aerospace-grade titanium and superalloy processing.

Today, the ESPON range of products is trusted by market leaders across India, South-East Asia, and the Middle East — a testament to four decades of applied research, field testing, and continuous improvement.

What We Stand For

Our Principles

The values that have guided four decades of product development and customer relationships.

Science-First Formulation

Every ESPON product is grounded in published metallurgy — oxidation kinetics, tribology, thermodynamics. We don't guess; we engineer.

Industrial Practicality

A coating that works in a lab but fails on the shop floor is worthless. All products are extensively field-tested under real production conditions before release.

Customer Partnership

We work alongside our customers to understand their specific processes, steel grades, and constraints — then recommend the right product, not the most expensive one.

Measurable Results

Scale reduction percentages. Die life increases. Heat loss savings. We believe product value should always be quantifiable and demonstrated clearly.

Responsible Chemistry

We actively reformulate to reduce hazardous components and improve biodegradability without compromising performance. Sustainability is an engineering challenge we embrace.

Open Knowledge

Our technical articles are freely available to all. Sharing metallurgical knowledge strengthens the entire industry — our customers, their customers, and the next generation of engineers.
