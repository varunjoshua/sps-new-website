Hot Forging

Presses & Hammers · Closed Die · Open Die

Hot forging involves controlled plastic deformation of heated metals into precision shapes under extreme pressure. Die cost represents 10–15% of total forging cost, making die life and lubrication critical to profitability. Clean, efficient forge shops also demand eco-friendly lubricants that eliminate graphite pollution.

Key Challenges
Scale loss during billet heating Up to 8% metal lost as scale, damaging die surfaces and reducing yield.
Die lubrication & temperature control Incorrect spray leads to over-heating, over-cooling, sticking, and short die life.
Graphite pollution in forge shops Black graphite deposits on floors, machinery, and in the air create hygiene and fire hazards.

Hot Rolling

Billets · Bars · Rails · Structural

In hot rolling, billets and ingots are heated to high temperatures before passing through rolling mills to achieve the desired shape. Scale formation, decarburisation, and billet sticking in furnaces are persistent problems that reduce yield, cause rejections, and damage expensive mill equipment.

Key Challenges
Scale formation & material loss Scale fallen in furnaces damages hearths and induction heater coils. Removal is labour-intensive.
Decarburisation in critical products Leaf springs, rails, and automotive parts require tight decarb control that is compromised at high temperatures.
Excess fuel consumption Worn refractory lining increases heat-up time and fuel use. Rolling mill furnaces lose efficiency within months.

Heat Treatment

Sealed Quench · Open Air · Induction

Heat treatment is critical to the mechanical properties of engineering components. Oxidation and decarburisation during open-air heat treatment lead to rejections, expensive post-processing, and wasted capital investment. SPS coatings allow scale-free heat treatment in open-air furnaces — eliminating the need for protective atmosphere equipment.

Key Challenges
Oxidation & scale in open-air furnaces Causes pit marks, dimensional loss, and rejected finish-machined components during re-heat treatment.
Furnace inefficiency over time Refractory lining wear increases heat-up time by up to 1 hour or more within 8–10 months of relining.
Costly gas atmosphere equipment Protective atmosphere furnaces require large capital investment. ESPON coatings eliminate this requirement.

Die Casting

Aluminium · Zinc · Magnesium · Copper Alloys

Die casting operations demand clean, well-maintained shop environments and reliable lubrication. Oil spills from centralised lubrication systems, dirty machinery, and poorly cleaned dies all contribute to quality issues and production downtime. SPS cleaning and lubrication products help die casting facilities maintain clean, efficient, and safe operations.

Key Challenges
Oil spills & floor contamination Accumulated oil on floors, machinery, and plastic trays creates safety, hygiene, and fire hazard risks.
Equipment cleaning downtime Conventional cleaning of oil-contaminated trays and bins requires high-pressure machines prone to filter choking.

Titanium & Super-Alloys

Titanium · Inconel · Critical Aerospace Alloys

High-performance alloys such as Titanium and Inconel are used in aerospace, defence, and critical engineering applications. These materials are expensive and highly sensitive to oxidation at forging and heat treatment temperatures. Alpha-case formation and oxidation losses make protective coatings essential — not optional.

Key Challenges
Alpha-case formation in Titanium Oxygen contamination at forging temperatures forms a brittle alpha-case layer that must be removed — a costly and lossy operation.
Heat retention during hot working Super-alloys lose temperature quickly. Protective coatings retain heat and extend the forging window.
